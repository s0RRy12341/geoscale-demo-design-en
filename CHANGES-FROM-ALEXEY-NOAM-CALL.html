The core problem from the call

Two products that needed to feel like one

There were two separate experiences: Inna's main Geoscale tool (per-domain scan dashboard, queries, reports, SEO/GEO research, content editor) and Nir's ScalePublish (external articles marketplace, agency / publisher two-sided flow). Alexei's complaint, almost word for word:

כלומר עכשיו צריך לשלב בין ה-UX שהוא עשה ל-UX הזה ככה שלא יהיה כפילויות במקומות, אבל כאילו בעצם שיהיה Flow כזה שיהיה שאילתות ומחקר וזה וזה וזה. יש Dashboard יפה, יש את כל מה שהוא עשה. פשוט כל הכפילויות בין הדברים. Alexei, call 1 · ~0:59
אבל צריכה להיות תוכנה אחת שלמה שיש בה גם את הפיצ'ר הזה של Publish במקום אחר. Alexei, call 1 · ~3:15
דבר שני שלא יהיה כל מיני כפילויות שיש, שיש שאילתות שנמצאות בתוכן ושאילתות שנמצאות לבד ושהיא כאילו שיהיה איזשהו UX שיהיה לא יהיה שם כפילויות שאילתות נמצאות שלוש פעמים במקומות שונים. Alexei, call 2 · ~2:34

Translation of the requirement: ScalePublish must become a sub-section of the main tool, accessible from inside the per-domain scan, sharing the same queries (no duplication), and items ordered there must flow into the agency's master client proposal as line items.

The 5 changes I shipped

What's different on the demo right now

Change 1 · /scan queries tab

Each query now has TWO action tracks side-by-side

When you expand any query in the Queries tab, you see two clearly-labeled cards under the AI engine answers:

  • Own site · Internal — the existing "Generate GEO content" / "Create content" CTA. Goes to /editor. This is Inna's flow, untouched.
  • Publisher · External — new CTA "Order article on Ynet, Calcalist, Sport5...". Deep-links into ScalePublish with the query pre-attached.

The split makes the difference between own-site GEO content and publisher placements explicit. Same query, two clearly different actions, no duplication.

Open /scan → Queries tab → expand any query →
Change 2 · /scan overview top-5 queries

Per-query "Order external" link in the Top 5 table

This is Noam's exact idea Alexei agreed with:

אם אתה עושה לך שם rank לחמש שאילתות העיקריות שמופיעות שם בדשבורד הראשי לצורך הדוגמה, אז כבר יהיה לך את האופציה ל-ללחוץ על אחת מהן, להיכנס ל-flow של קניית תכנים ולבחור, אה, להוסיף לסל כתבה על אותו שאילתה. Noam, call 2 · ~6:00

Done. Each row in the Top 5 Queries table on the overview tab now has an "Order external →" link in a new Action column. One click → ScalePublish opens with that query pre-attached and the Order Flow builder already loaded.

Open /scan → Overview tab → scroll to Top 5 queries →
Change 3 · ScalePublish entry experience

Deep-link auto-jumps into the article builder

When you arrive at /scale-publish?source=domain&queryText=... the page now:

  • Switches to Agency view automatically (regardless of last-saved mode)
  • Finds the matching query in the system, pre-selects it (5-of-5 selection counter shows 1)
  • Switches to the Order Flow tab so the agency lands in the article-building step, not on a list
  • Toast confirms: "Query 'X' attached — building the article"

Previously: deep-link landed on My Queries with a silent selection 9 rows down — agency had to hunt for it. Now: agency lands in the builder ready to pick sections.

Change 4 · Source-context breadcrumb

"External content for [domain]" strip at the top

When deep-linked from a scan, ScalePublish shows a green left-border strip above the agency tabs with the domain favicon, brand name, a one-line explanation that orders here flow into the client proposal, and a "← Back to [domain] scan" button. This makes ScalePublish visually feel like a sub-section of the scan, not a separate app.

Change 5 · Order submit copy

Submit button reframed as a proposal line-item

Old: "Submit order to Yedioth Ahronoth" with caption "Yedioth's sales team will contact your agency to confirm and collect payment."

New: "Add to client proposal & send to Yedioth" with caption "This becomes a line item on your master client proposal." Matches Alexei's exact framing:

מוסיף את זה להצעת מחיר כשהוא מוסיף את זה להצעת מחיר... זה גם מוסיף את זה להצעת מחיר של הלקוח בסוף. הבנת? של Agency. Alexei, call 2 · ~0:59

Try it yourself

3-step path to see the merge end-to-end

  1. Open /scan. You're now inside a per-domain scan dashboard (the "Dashboard" Alexei was referring to). Scroll to "Top 5 queries" — see the new "Order external →" links on the right.
  2. Click one of those links. ScalePublish opens, you see the green "External content for [domain]" strip up top with a "← Back to scan" button, and you're already inside the Order Flow with that query attached. Pick sections, build the article.
  3. Hit "Add to client proposal & send to Yedioth" at the bottom. The caption tells you it's a line item on the master proposal. Toast confirms. Toggle to Publisher view → the order is in the inbox with the source query and the full article preview.

Alternative path: per-query CTA

From /scan, click the Queries tab, expand any query. You'll see two side-by-side action cards: Own site · Internal on the left (Inna's existing /editor flow) and Publisher · External on the right (new ScalePublish deep-link). Click the orange one — same end result.

Still open

What I deliberately did NOT change in this round

Visual redesign (Google Analytics / SimilarWeb feel)

הוא שם שם הרבה מאוד boldים, הרבה מאוד דברים שדשבורדים לא נראים ככה. שילך ל-ל reference טוב לגוגל אנליטיקס. Alexei, call 2 · ~3:00

This is a real ask — the demo is more colorful and bold than a dashboard product should be. It needs:

  • Lighter typography weights (less 800-bold, more 500-600)
  • Reduced color palette — fewer simultaneous accent colors per screen
  • More table-style data presentation, fewer KPI hero cards
  • Tighter information density, less hero-banner real estate

I held off because (a) it's a 4-8 hour pass that touches every screen, and (b) the merge logic in this round is the bigger architectural unblock. Recommend doing the visual cleanup as the next sprint, with SimilarWeb's UI pulled up alongside as the reference.

Master-proposal-side: agency zero-base service line

Alexei mentioned that the agency could put zero on its own service price and only sell articles. That logic lives on the master proposal side (DealroomJIT or whichever proposal builder we're integrating with), not inside ScalePublish itself. The hand-off from ScalePublish to the proposal is now framed correctly ("line item on master proposal") — the zero-base toggle lives in the proposal tool.

ScalePublish My Queries source-of-truth

When deep-linked from /scan, we now skip the My Queries list entirely (auto-jump to Order Flow). When the agency opens ScalePublish standalone (not from a scan), they still see the demo Bank Hapoalim queries. Long-term: My Queries should pull queries directly from the brand scan instead of having its own demo list. Doable in a follow-up; not blocking the merge story.
